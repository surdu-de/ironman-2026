### Key Learning: Garmin Coach Limitations
**Decision:** Moving away from Garmin Coach prescribed workouts for future weeks

**Concrete Issues Identified:**

1. **Tuesday Sprint Workout (32x 10s sprints)**
   - Target: Sweetspot 237W (108% FTP) sustained work
   - Actual: 138W average (63% FTP) - too many short bursts with long recoveries
   - Structure failure: 10s sprints don't build Ironman-specific sustained power
   - Result: Recovery ride intensity when threshold work was needed

2. **Wednesday Sprint Workout (12x 10s sprints)**
   - Similar pattern: 110W average, sprint peaks only 268W
   - 2-minute recovery periods between 10s efforts = insufficient training load
   - Too easy for intended purpose

3. **Custom Workout Success (10x 10s max efforts)**
   - Designed immediately after disappointing Garmin session
   - Achieved proper anaerobic stimulus: 468W average per sprint (213% FTP)
   - Peak power 676W (307% FTP)
   - Shorter 50s recoveries created cumulative fatigue (HR climbed to 165)
   - Demonstrated that self-designed intervals achieve training goals

**Core Problem:** Garmin prescriptions favor short sprint intervals over sustained efforts. Ironman requires:
- Sustained sweetspot/threshold work (20-60min intervals)
- Tempo endurance (60-180min Z3)
- Not sprint capacity training

**Solution for Week 5+:** Design custom workouts targeting:
- Long sweetspot intervals (2x20min, 3x15min @ 95-105% FTP)
- Threshold builds (40-60min progressive efforts @ 85-95% FTP)
- Tempo endurance rides (2-4h @ 70-80% FTP)
- Run threshold intervals aligned with Ironman pacing strategy

This realization during Week 4 (recovery/testing week) is perfectly timed - entering Week 5 (start of new build cycle) with clear direction for targeted, self-designed training sessions.
